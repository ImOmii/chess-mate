About

"CHESSBlog It’s the first week back at uni for the new semester and it’s time to get feedback on our MVP with our module tutor and mentor. Walking into the meeting we realistically did not expect much positive points or a positive grade for the MVP as we did have a technical malfunction. Regardless of the fact that this was not our final grade, everyone on the team strives to do the best we possibly can so you can imagine the excitement for this meeting was non-existent. To our surprise, our feedback was better than we expected. As we did in fact have a working MVP but was unable to demo it during the presentation, this was enough to warrant us a pass as well as how we handled the situation where the technical error happened. We remained calm and got through as best as we could, trying to fill the void left by the technical fault. This uplifted our mood drastically and motivated us to resume our work on the game, most importantly assured us to not make the same mistake and come back with the best possible game we can produce."
